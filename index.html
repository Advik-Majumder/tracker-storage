Behaviour & Cleanliness Tracker

2026–27 · weekly

What this app does

This app works out the weekly Behaviour and Cleanliness score for each class from its paper tracker sheet. You take a photo of the sheet, the app reads the ticks for you, you check it, and it gives you two percentages. You do this once for each of the 24 classes every week.

1 Pick the class and check the week

At the top of Enter sheet, choose the class whose sheet you are holding from the Class dropdown. It starts empty on purpose, so you always choose consciously — you cannot save without picking one.

The Week no. fills in automatically from today's date. You only change it if you are entering a sheet for a different week.

2 Read the sheet from a photo

Tap Take / choose photo. Your phone lets you either take a new photo or pick one from your gallery. Take a clear, flat photo of the whole sheet in good light, with no shadow or blur.

You will see a preview. If it looks good, tap Read this sheet. Wait a few seconds — the app is reading all the ticks. If the photo is bad, tap Choose a different photo and try again.

Tip: you don't have to use a photo at all. You can fill the sheet in by hand instead (see step 4). The photo is just there to save time.

3 Check the result carefully

The grid fills in automatically with what the app read — green for Good, amber for Okay, red for Bad, and empty for blank (unsigned) rows.

Some rows may be highlighted in orange. These are rows the app was not fully sure about. A banner at the top tells you how many. Look at each highlighted row and compare it with the paper:

  • If it is wrong, tap the correct face to fix it.
  • If it is correct (including a correct blank row), tap the orange ✓ on that row to confirm it.
  • Or use Confirm all as correct in the banner if every highlighted row is fine.

You can change any row at any time, not just the highlighted ones — just tap a face to set it, or tap it again to clear it back to blank.

4 Marking by hand (no photo)

If you'd rather not use a photo, just click the faces directly to match the paper sheet. Each row has Good, Okay and Bad. A row you leave untouched counts as blank. This works exactly the same and costs nothing.

5 Read the score and save

At the bottom of the screen, two cards always show the live Behaviour % and Cleanliness %, with a breakdown of how many Good, Okay, Bad and Blank there are. The score updates as you make changes.

When you are happy, scroll to the bottom and tap Save & next ›. This saves the score for that class and that week. The class dropdown then resets to empty, ready for the next sheet.

If a class already has a score saved for that week, it asks before overwriting — so you never get duplicates.

6 See and download all results

Go to the Results tab. Pick a week, and you'll see all 24 classes grouped by grade, with each one's Behaviour % and Cleanliness %. Classes you haven't done yet show a dash (—). The chip tells you how many of 24 are done.

Tap Download Excel to save that week's table as a spreadsheet file — this is how you share the results with the school and keep a record.

Important things to remember

  • Use one device for the whole week. Scores are saved on the device you're using, not in a shared place. Do all the sheets for a week on the same phone or laptop.
  • Always download the Excel file after finishing a week. That's your safe copy. If the browser data is ever cleared, anything not downloaded is lost.
  • Always check before saving. The app reads the sheet, but you confirm it. A quick look at the highlighted rows is all it takes.

How the score is worked out

Each mark is worth points: Good = 3, Okay = 2, Bad = 1, Blank = 0. The percentage is the points earned out of the maximum possible (every slot counted as if it were Good).

This means blank (unsigned) periods lower the score — they count in the total but earn nothing. Behaviour is out of 50 periods a week; Cleanliness is out of 10. The two are worked out completely separately.

Read from photo (optional)

Take or choose a clear, flat photo of the whole sheet in bright, even light. Use flash and hold the phone at a slight angle to avoid shadow and glare. You'll preview it, then review the result before it's saved.
